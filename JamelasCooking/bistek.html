Bistek Tagalog

Bistek is a common staple in the Tagalog and Western Visayan regions of the Philippines and is eaten over white rice. Bistek comes from the Spanish word bistec or beefsteak. The dish is also known as Bistek Tagalog or Karne Frita, which consists of thinly-sliced beefsteak braised in soy sauce, calamansi juice, garlic, ground black pepper, and onions.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs beef sirloin thinly sliced
  • 5 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 pieces calamansi or 1-piece lemon
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 cloves garlic minced
  • 3 pieces yellow onion sliced into rings
  • 4 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 pinch salt
