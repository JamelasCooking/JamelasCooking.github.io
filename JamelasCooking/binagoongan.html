Binagoongan

Binagoongan is meat saut√©ed or braised in bagoong alamang shrimp paste and some recipes also add pineapples, chilis, or coconut cream to balance the flavors. It is very similar to Pinatisan which is cooked with fish sauce, a by-product of fermenting bagoong. The dish is characteristically quite salty with a strong flavor, which is why it is always paired with white rice and never eaten on its own.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. bagnet (crispy pork belly), sliced into serving pieces
  • 2 pieces eggplant sliced
  • 1/4 cup bagoong alamang (shrimp paste)
  • 2 pieces Serrano pepper sliced
  • 1 piece tomato chopped
  • 1 piece onion minced
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon granulated white sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
