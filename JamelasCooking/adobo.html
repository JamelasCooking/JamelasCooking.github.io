Adobo

Adobo was traditionally cooked in clay pots but is made in more common metal pots or woks today. When the Spanish invaded and settled in the Philippines during the 16th century, they witnessed this traditional Filipino cooking method and called it adobo, the Spanish word for marinade.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 6 cloves garlic crushed
  • 1 pc onion sliced
  • 1 kilogram chicken cut ups
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 pcs bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns (slightly crushed)
  • 1 tsp brown sugar packed
  • Option: 1 cup kale or spinach
