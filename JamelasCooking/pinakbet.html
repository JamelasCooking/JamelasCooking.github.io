Pinakbet

Pinakbet is more than just a regional cuisine. It is a symbol of the Ilocano palate. It originated in the region of Ilocos, but it appears in many regional and seasonal varieties. The word pinakbet is derived from the Ilocano word Pinakebet, meaning shrunk or shriveled.

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 pound pork loin, chopped
  • 1/2 pound peeled and deveined prawns
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 pound pumpkin, seeded and cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1/4 pound fresh okra, ends trimmed
  • 1/4 pound fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 1/4 pound eggplant, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1 small bitter melon, cut into bite-size pieces
